Abstract

Recently, Neural Radiance Field(NeRF) is widely used for large-scale Novel view synthesis. However, as lightning variation or moving objects exist in the real world scenarios, existing methods tend to cause to floater or blur problems in novel view synthesis. To address these issues, we propose CaRT-NeLF, which harnesses local core appearance embedding with several regularization techniques. We optimize local core appearance embedding for each local core probe to mitigate floater or blur, and design aggregation module through empirical experiments to enrich multi-channel features. In addition to, we adapt several regularization to generalize unseen views. In particular, we regularize similar distributions of weight values among neighboring rays. Furthermore, we exploit lipshitz MLP to stabilize the training process. Through extensive experiments across small-scale and large-scale on several benchmark datasets, we demonstrate performance improvements of our method over existing approaches. The source code will be made publicly available.
